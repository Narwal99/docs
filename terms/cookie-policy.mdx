This Cookie Policy explains how GPUhub (“Company,” “we,” “us,” and “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our website at [GPUhub](https://gpuhub.com/). It explains what these technologies are and why we use them, as well as your rights to control our use of them.

## What are cookies?

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work, or to work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information.

## What are first-party and third-party cookies?

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, GPUhub) are called “first-party cookies.” Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called “third-party cookies.” Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through the website (e.g., advertising, interactive content, and analytics). The parties that set these third-party cookies can recognize your computer both when it visits the website in question and also when it visits certain other websites.

## What are session and persistent cookies?

Session cookies are cookies that allow website operators to link the actions of a user during a browser session. A browser session starts when a user opens the browser window and finishes when they close the browser window. Session cookies are created temporarily. Once you close the browser, all session cookies are deleted.

Persistent cookies are cookies that remain on a user's device for the period of time specified in the cookie. They are activated each time that the user visits the Website that created that particular cookie.

## What categories of cookies do we use?
We use the following categories of cookies:

- **Strictly Necessary Cookies**: These cookies are essential for the proper functioning and security of our Website. They enable you to move around the site and use its features. Without these cookies, some parts of our Website may not work as intended.
- **Performance Cookies**: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Website. They provide information about how visitors use the site, which pages are visited, and any error messages encountered. This allows us to improve the performance and user experience of our Website.
- **Functional Cookies**: These cookies enable additional functionalities and personalization of our Website. They may be used to remember your preferences, settings, or login information. This allows for a more customized and convenient experience when using our site.
- **Targeting Cookies**: These cookies are used to deliver content and advertisements that are more relevant to your interests. They track your browsing habits and may share this information with third parties for advertising purposes.

## Why do we use cookies?

We use cookies for several important reasons:

- To ensure the technical operation and security of our Website.
- To enhance the user experience by providing personalized content and features.
- To analyze how our Website is used and to improve its performance.
- To deliver targeted advertising that may be of interest to you.

## How can I control cookies on my browser?

You can manage your cookie preferences through your web browser settings. Most browsers allow you to accept, reject, or delete cookies. Here are some general steps you can follow:
Open your web browser.

1. Locate and click on the settings or options menu (usually represented by three dots or lines).
2. Find the privacy or security settings section.
3. Look for an option related to cookies or site data.
4. Choose your preferred cookie settings.
5. Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect the functionality of some parts of our Website.

## How long do cookies stay on my device?

The duration of cookies on your device varies depending on the type and purpose of the cookie. Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a longer period, as specified in the cookie.
